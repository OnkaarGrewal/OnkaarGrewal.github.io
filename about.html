Sport

Volleyball

I've been playing volleyball for several years and it's the sport that taught me how to function under pressure. A bad serve doesn't end the rally — you reset, communicate, and keep moving.

There's a systems-thinking aspect to the game that I find genuinely satisfying: reading the opponent's formation, anticipating the next move, and making decisions faster than you can consciously reason through them.

"Team sports teach you that your worst day is someone else's problem to solve — together."

Climbing

Rock Climbing

Climbing is the most direct analogy to engineering I've found outside of a lab. You look at a problem (the wall), form a hypothesis (the route), try it, fail, and iterate until it works.

It also demands a lot of patience with yourself. Some routes take days or weeks of attempts. You learn to separate frustration from failure — one is an emotion, the other is data.

"Every route is a puzzle. Physics decides if your solution is correct."

Hobby

Photography

Photography made me better at noticing things. It's easy to walk past interesting geometry, light, and texture when you're not looking for it. A camera forces the question: what is actually here?
